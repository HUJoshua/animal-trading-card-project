Kea

The kea bird is the only alpine parrot that exists in the world

  • Scientific Name: Nestor notabilis
  • Average Length: 48 centimetes
  • Average Lifespan: 5 years
  • Habitat: Lowland river valleys, coastal forests and alpine regions

kea are large parrots found only in the South Island of Zealand. They have dark green feathers, marked with either brown or gray. On the underside of their wings, they have bright orange feathers which is typically seen when the bird is in flight. They have large beaks, which curve to a sharp point.

Angora Rabbit

Angora rabbits need to eat a lot of hay to help them digest the fur they ingest while grooming themselves

  • Scientific Name: Oryctolagus cuniculus domesticus
  • Weight: 2.0-5.5 kg
  • Lifespan: 7-12 years
  • Natural Habitat: Meadows, priaries, deserts, farmlands, thickets, forests, and wetlands

Rabbits of the Angora breed are known for their beautiful appearance. They are calm and friendly in nature. These rabbits are adorned with fur. Their entire body -including the face- is covered with wool. The only wool-free areas are the feet and the area above the nose. They are widely used as pet and are also used for Angora wool production.
